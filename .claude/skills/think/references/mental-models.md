# Thinking Toolkit

Three tiers: **Lenses** (what to notice), **Operations** (what to do), **Recipes** (multi-step procedures for problem archetypes).

Lenses reveal what to act on. Operations produce conclusions, hypotheses, or tests. Recipes chain Operations through Lenses for specific problem types.

---

## Lenses (12)

Lenses direct attention to patterns that are invisible without them. They're observational filters, not actions.

| # | Lens | What It Surfaces | Key Question |
|---|------|-----------------|--------------|
| 1 | **Anchoring** | First-number/first-option bias | "What did you see first, and how much is it still shaping the frame?" |
| 2 | **Loss Aversion** | Asymmetric risk perception (losses feel 2x worse) | "What's the cost of NOT changing over the next 12 months?" |
| 3 | **Social Proof** | Herd influence disguised as evidence | "Would this still be the right choice if nobody else were doing it?" |
| 4 | **Confirmation Bias** | Selective evidence seeking | "What's the strongest evidence against your current direction, and have you actually looked for it?" |
| 5 | **Status Quo Bias** | Change resistance masquerading as stability | "If you were starting fresh today, would you build the current setup?" |
| 6 | **Sunk Cost** | Past investment anchoring future decisions | "If you had no history here, would you choose this?" |
| 7 | **Opportunity Cost** | Hidden tradeoffs in every choice | "What are you NOT doing because you're doing this?" |
| 8 | **Map vs Territory** | Model-reality gaps | "When reality doesn't match the plan, and it won't, what breaks first?" |
| 9 | **Feedback Loops** | Amplifying or stabilizing cycles | "Which loops are running here, amplifying or stabilizing, and is that what you want?" |
| 10 | **Bottleneck** | The single constraint limiting throughput | "What's the one thing that, if fixed, would move everything else?" |
| 11 | **Leverage Points** | Where small changes produce large effects | "Where would a 10% change produce a 50% outcome shift?" |
| 12 | **Margin of Safety** | Buffer adequacy against uncertainty | "What's your plan if the estimate is 30% wrong?" |

**When to reach for a Lens:** You need to NOTICE something in a situation before deciding what to do about it. Lenses are diagnostic. They don't produce action. They reveal what to act on.

## Operations (17)

Operations are active reasoning procedures. Each one transforms observations into a specific type of insight.

### Generate (5) — Create new possibilities

| # | Operation | What It Does | Signature Question |
|---|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Second-Order Thinking** | Chase consequences 3-4 levels deep. "And then what?" | "If this works exactly as planned, what problem does it create 3 steps from now?" |
| 2 | **Information-Revelation** | Design small experiments to reveal info before bigger bets | "What's the smallest experiment that would tell you what to do next?" |
| 3 | **Analogical Reasoning** | Import structures from distant, unrelated domains | "What field has already solved a structurally similar problem?" |
| 4 | **Abductive Reasoning** | Generate hypotheses explaining surprising observations | "What would have to be true to make this observation non-surprising?" |
| 5 | **Counterfactual Analysis** | Isolate factors by imagining their absence | "What would change, and what wouldn't, if this one factor were different?" |

### Evaluate (7) — Test a position

| # | Operation | What It Does | Signature Question |
|---|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| 6 | **Pre-Mortem** | Imagine complete failure from the future | "It's one year from now and this failed. What's the most likely reason?" |
| 7 | **Reversibility Check** | Classify: one-way door or two-way door | "If this turns out wrong, how exactly do you undo it?" |
| 8 | **Regret Minimization** | Project to age 80 | "When you're 80, which choice will you regret not making?" |
| 9 | **Falsification** | Actively try to prove your best idea wrong | "What evidence would prove this wrong? Does it exist?" |
| 10 | **Bayesian Updating** | Calibrate confidence to actual evidence | "Given this new evidence, how much should my confidence actually change?" |
| 11 | **Base Rate Check** | Ground in statistics, not gut feel | "What's the actual base rate for situations like this succeeding?" |
| 12 | **Null Hypothesis** | Define what would disprove the direction, then look for it | "What evidence would make you abandon this, and have you looked?" |

### Deconstruct (2) — Break down the problem

| # | Operation | What It Does | Signature Question |
|---|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| 13 | **Inversion** | Flip the problem. Guarantee failure, then avoid those things. | "What actions would guarantee this fails?" |
| 14 | **First Principles** | Strip assumptions, rebuild from fundamentals. "Zeroth Principle": question what the problem IS, not just how to solve it. | "What would I believe about this with zero prior knowledge?" |

### Integrate (3) — Synthesize competing views

| # | Operation | What It Does | Signature Question |
|---|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| 15 | **Dialectical Synthesis** | Hold opposing positions, find the higher-order truth | "What becomes visible ONLY when I take both sides seriously?" |
| 16 | **Systems Thinking** | Map relationships, feedback loops, emergent properties | "What does this connect to that nobody is tracking?" |
| 17 | **Perspective Simulation** | Model the smartest opposing advocate | "What would the smartest critic say, and would they be right?" |

**When to reach for an Operation:** You've noticed something (via a Lens or conversation) and need to DO something with that observation.

**Selection by need:**
- Need to GENERATE new possibilities → Operations 1-5
- Need to EVALUATE a position → Operations 6-12
- Need to DECONSTRUCT a problem → Operations 13-14
- Need to INTEGRATE competing views → Operations 15-17

## Recipes (5)

Recipes are multi-step procedures: specific Operations run through specific Lenses in a defined sequence. Each targets a problem archetype.

### Recipe 1: Wrong-Problem Detector

**When:** Stuck on something. Suspect you might be solving the wrong problem.

| Step | Operation | Through Lens | What It Produces |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | First Principles (Zeroth Principle) | via Inversion | Question the assumptions BEHIND the assumptions about what the problem even is |
| 2 | Abductive Reasoning | — | Given the pattern of failure, what alternative problem would make that failure non-surprising? |
| 3 | Counterfactual Analysis | — | If the blocker were removed, would you actually progress, or does it serve a function? |
| 4 | Falsification | — | Design a test: what would you observe if the new framing is correct vs incorrect? |

### Recipe 2: Blind Spot Finder

**When:** Working on something and suspect you're missing something important.

| Step | Operation | Through Lens | What It Produces |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Systems Thinking | Feedback Loops, Leverage Points | Second-order effects, hidden incentives, invisible feedback loops |
| 2 | Perspective Simulation | — | Strongest case for the position you're most tempted to dismiss |
| 3 | Abductive Reasoning | — | Are your metrics measuring what matters? What should be present that isn't? |
| 4 | Analogical Reasoning | — | Historical cases from other fields where this blind spot caused damage |

### Recipe 3: Innovation Engine

**When:** Need a genuinely novel approach, not incremental improvement.

| Step | Operation | Through Lens | What It Produces |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Analogical Reasoning | — | Structural parallels from 3 completely unrelated domains |
| 2 | First Principles | — | Core operating principle from the most promising analogy |
| 3 | Dialectical Synthesis | — | What emerges from holding that principle against real constraints |
| 4 | Pre-Mortem | — | If implemented and failed, what went wrong? |

### Recipe 4: Decision Stress Test

**When:** Decision is made or nearly made. Need to find the cracks before reality does. Primary recipe for Decision Audit tier.

| Step | Operation | Through Lens | What It Produces |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Null Hypothesis | — | What evidence would disprove this? Have you looked? |
| 2 | Base Rate Check | — | Actual success rate for this type of situation |
| 3 | Pre-Mortem | Loss Aversion, Sunk Cost | Is the failure story about the decision or about a bias? |
| 4 | Perspective Simulation | — | Strongest opposing case. Does it survive cross-examination? |

### Recipe 5: Builder's Trap Check

**When:** Deep in infrastructure/tooling work. Check if building is the right move or just comfortable. ([Your Name]-specific.)

| Step | Operation | Through Lens | What It Produces |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Inversion | Opportunity Cost | What are you NOT doing because you're doing this? |
| 2 | Counterfactual Analysis | — | If this infrastructure didn't exist, what's the simplest thing that works? |
| 3 | Base Rate Check | — | How often does this type of infrastructure actually get used after being built? |
| 4 | Reversibility Check | — | Can you build this later if you need it, or is there a now-or-never reason? |

## Selection Guide

How to navigate the taxonomy during advisory sessions:

1. **Reversibility Check** runs first on every decision
2. **Lens scan:** Which Lenses reveal something non-obvious about this situation? Apply 1-2. Use Key Questions to probe.
3. **Operation selection:** Based on what Lenses surfaced, what reasoning move is needed?
   - Need new possibilities → Generate operations
   - Need to test a position → Evaluate operations
   - Need to break down the problem → Deconstruct operations
   - Need to integrate competing views → Integrate operations
4. **Recipe check:** Does this match a known problem archetype?
   - Stuck / wrong problem → Recipe 1: Wrong-Problem Detector
   - Suspect blind spots → Recipe 2: Blind Spot Finder
   - Need novelty → Recipe 3: Innovation Engine
   - Decision made, stress-test → Recipe 4: Decision Stress Test
   - Deep in building, check yourself → Recipe 5: Builder's Trap Check
