## IRA & Retirement Contribution Limits

*WebSearch "2025 IRA contribution limits" and "2025 solo 401k limits" before using.*

**IRA (Traditional + Roth combined, 2024):**
- Under age 50: $7,000/year
- Age 50+: $8,000/year (catch-up)
- Roth IRA phase-out begins at $146,000 MAGI (single, 2024)

**Solo 401(k) (2024):**
- Employee contribution (elective deferral): up to $23,000 (or 100% of net SE income, whichever is less)
- Age 50+ catch-up: additional $7,500
- Employer contribution (profit-sharing): up to 25% of net SE income
- Combined limit (employee + employer): $69,000 (or $76,500 if 50+)
- **Requires LLC to have business revenue** — $0 revenue = $0 allowable contribution

**SEP-IRA (alternative, simpler):** 25% of net SE income, max $69,000. No employee contribution. Easier to administer than solo 401(k) but less contribution room.

## Self-Employment Tax

**Rate:** 15.3% on 92.35% of net self-employment income (i.e., effective rate ~14.1% on gross SE income)
- 12.4% Social Security (on income up to Social Security wage base ~$168,600 in 2024)
- 2.9% Medicare (no cap)
- Additional 0.9% Medicare surcharge on SE income over $200,000 (single)

**Deduction:** 50% of SE tax is deductible from gross income (not SE income). Reduces federal income tax, not SE tax itself.

**When this kicks in:** Any net LLC profit triggers SE tax. Even $1 of Schedule C profit. This is the first major tax cost when [Your Company] generates revenue.

## Estimated Quarterly Payments

**Required when:** Expected tax liability ≥ $1,000 after withholding. With no employer withholding, any meaningful income triggers quarterly payments.

**Schedule (standard):**
| Payment | Covers           | Due Date    |
|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| Q1      | Jan 1 – Mar 31  | April 15    |
| Q2      | Apr 1 – May 31  | June 15     |
| Q3      | Jun 1 – Aug 31  | September 15|
| Q4      | Sep 1 – Dec 31  | January 15 (following year) |

**Safe harbor rule:** Pay 100% of prior year's tax liability (110% if prior year AGI > $150K) and no underpayment penalty applies, even if you owe more at filing.

**Texas:** No state income tax. No state estimated payments.

## Roth Conversion Ladder

**The opportunity:** Convert traditional IRA funds to Roth IRA during years with low or zero taxable income. Pay income tax now (at current low/zero rate) instead of later (at higher rate in retirement).

**How it works:**
1. Choose conversion amount
2. Converted amount is added to ordinary income for the year
3. Pay income tax at current bracket
4. 5-year waiting period before penalty-free withdrawal of converted principal (age 59½ rule separate)
5. Earnings grow tax-free forever

**[Your Name]'s golden window:** Zero business income + no employment income = taxable income near $0. Standard deduction ($14,600 in 2024) means first ~$14,600 of conversion is taxed at 0%. Next ~$11,600 is taxed at 10%. Next ~$35,550 at 12%. Annual conversion of ~$47K at very low blended rate.

**ACA interaction — CRITICAL:** Roth conversions increase MAGI. If [Your Name] is on an ACA marketplace plan, higher MAGI reduces or eliminates the premium tax credit. Model the net tax impact (conversion savings minus lost ACA credit) before recommending a conversion amount.

**Roth conversion vs capital gains harvesting:** Both compete for the same "low income bracket space" in a given year. Don't double-fill the bracket. Choose which to prioritize based on: (a) whether gains are already unrealized in taxable brokerage, and (b) long-term tax exposure in traditional IRA.

## ACA Premium Tax Credit

**MAGI cliff warning:** The ACA premium tax credit phases out as MAGI increases. In some brackets, a $1 increase in MAGI can reduce the credit by more than $1 (effective marginal rate > 100% in the phase-out zone). Always check current-year MAGI thresholds before recommending Roth conversions or capital gains harvesting.

WebSearch: "2025 ACA premium tax credit income limits" for current thresholds.

## Social Security

**Full retirement age (FRA):** 67 for those born 1960 or later.

**Bend points (2024 — verify current year):**
- 90% of AIME up to $1,174/month
- 32% of AIME between $1,174 and $7,078
- 15% of AIME above $7,078

**Early claiming:** Reduced benefit at 62 (up to 30% reduction). Delayed claiming: +8%/year up to age 70.

**Earnings history gap:** Years with zero earnings lower the AIME. [Your Name]'s career transition means recent zero-income years will reduce the benefit calculation. Get an actual SSA estimate from ssa.gov — don't rely on training data for the specific number.

**For projections:** Use SSA statement value as the input. Flag if more than 2 years old (earnings history has changed).
